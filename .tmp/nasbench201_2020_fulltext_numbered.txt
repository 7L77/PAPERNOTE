00009: ABSTRACT
00010: Neural architecture search (NAS) has achieved breakthrough success in a great
00011: number of applications in the past few years. It could be time to take a step back
00012: and analyze the good and bad aspects in the ямБeld of NAS. A variety of algorithms
00013: search architectures under different search space. These searched architectures are
00014: trained using different setups, e.g., hyper-parameters, data augmentation, regular-
00015: ization. This raises a comparability problem when comparing the performance
00016: of various NAS algorithms. NAS-Bench-101 has shown success to alleviate this
00017: problem. In this work, we propose an extension to NAS-Bench-101: NAS-Bench-
00018: 201 with a different search space, results on multiple datasets, and more diagnos-
00019: tic information. NAS-Bench-201 has a ямБxed search space and provides a uniямБed
00020: benchmark for almost any up-to-date NAS algorithms. The design of our search
00021: space is inspired from the one used in the most popular cell-based searching al-
00022: gorithms, where a cell is represented as a directed acyclic graph. Each edge here
00023: is associated with an operation selected from a predeямБned operation set. For it to
00024: be applicable for all NAS algorithms, the search space deямБned in NAS-Bench-201
00025: includes all possible architectures generated by 4 nodes and 5 associated operation
00026: options, which results in 15,625 neural cell candidates in total. The training log
00027: using the same setup and the performance for each architecture candidate are pro-
00028: vided for three datasets. This allows researchers to avoid unnecessary repetitive
00029: training for selected architecture and focus solely on the search algorithm itself.
00030: The training time saved for every architecture also largely improves the efямБciency
00031: of most NAS algorithms and brings a more computational cost friendly NAS com-
00032: munity for a broader range of researchers. We provide additional diagnostic in-
00033: formation such as ямБne-grained loss and accuracy, which can give inspirations to
00034: new designs of NAS algorithms. In further support of the proposed NAS-Bench-
00035: 201, we have analyzed it from many aspects and benchmarked 10 recent NAS
00036: algorithms, which verify its applicability.

00131: 2 NAS-Bench-201
00132: Our NAS-Bench-201 is algorithm-agnostic. Put simply, it is applicable to almost any up-to-date NAS
00133: algorithms. In this section, we will brieямВy introduce our NAS-Bench-201. The search space of NAS-
00134: Bench-201 is inspired by cell-based NAS algorithms (Section 2.1). NAS-Bench-201 evaluates each
00135: architecture on three different datasets (Section 2.2). All implementation details of NAS-Bench-201

00138: 2.1 A RCHITECTURES IN THE SEARCH SPACE
00139: Macro Skeleton. Our search space follows the design of its counterpart as used in the recent neural
00140: cell-based NAS algorithms (Liu et al., 2019; Zoph et al., 2018; Pham et al., 2018). As shown in the
00141: top of Figure 1, the skeleton is initiated with one 3-by-3 convolution with 16 output channels and
00142: a batch normalization layer (Ioffe & Szegedy, 2015). The main body of the skeleton includes three
00143: stacks of cells, connected by a residual block. Each cell is stacked N = 5 times, with the number of
00144: output channels as 16, 32 and 64 for the ямБrst, second and third stages, respectively. The intermediate
00145: residual block is the basic residual block with a stride of 2 (He et al., 2016), which serves to down-
00146: sample the spatial size and double the channels of an input feature map. The shortcut path in this
00147: residual block consists of a 2-by-2 average pooling layer with stride of 2 and a 1-by-1 convolution.
00148: The skeleton ends up with a global average pooling layer to ямВatten the feature map into a feature
00149: vector. ClassiямБcation uses a fully connected layer with a softmax layer to transform the feature
00150: vector into the ямБnal prediction.
00151: Searched Cell. Each cell in the search space is represented as a densely connected DAG. The
00152: densely connected DAG is obtained by assigning a direction from the i-th node to the j-th node
00153: (i < j ) for each edge in an undirected complete graph. Each edge in this DAG is associated with
00154: an operation transforming the feature map from the source node to the target node. All possible
00155: operations are selected from a predeямБned operation set, as shown in Figure 1(bottom-right). In our
00156: NAS-Bench-201, the predeямБned operation set O has L = 5 representative operations: (1) zeroize,
00157: (2) skip connection, (3) 1-by-1 convolution, (4) 3-by-3 convolution, and (5) 3-by-3 average pooling
00158: layer. The convolution in this operation set is an abbreviation of an operation sequence of ReLU,
00159: convolution, and batch normalization. The DAG has V = 4 nodes, where each node represents the
00160: sum of all feature maps transformed through the associated operations of the edges pointing to this
00161: node. We choose V = 4 to allow the search space to contain basic residual block-like cells, which
00162: requires 4 nodes. Densely connected DAG does not restrict the searched topology of the cell to be
00163: densely connected, since we include zeroize in the operation set, which is an operation of dropping
00164: the associated edge. Besides, since we do not impose the constraint on the maximum number of
00165: edges (Ying et al., 2019), our search space is applicable to most NAS algorithms, including all
00166: cell-based NAS algorithms.
00167: 2.2 D ATASETS
00168: We train and evaluate each architecture on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 (Krizhevsky et al., 2009), and
00169: ImageNet-16-120 (Chrabaszcz et al., 2017). We choose these three datasets because CIFAR and
00170: ImageNet (Russakovsky et al., 2015) are the most popular image classiямБcation datasets.
00171: We split each dataset into training, validation and test sets to provide a consistent training and evalu-
00172: ation settings for previous NAS algorithms (Liu et al., 2019). Most NAS methods use the validation
00173: set to evaluate architectures after the architecture is optimized on the training set. The validation
00174: performance of the architectures serves as supervision signals to update the searching algorithm.
00175: The test set is to evaluate the performance of each searching algorithm by comparing the indicators
00176: (e.g., accuracy, model size, speed) of their selected architectures. Previous methods use different
00177: splitting strategies, which may result in various searching costs and unfair comparisons. We hope to
00178: use the proposed splits to unify the training, validation and test sets for a fairer comparison.

00280: 2.4 D IAGNOSTIC INFORMATION
00281: Validation accuracy is a commonly used supervision signal for NAS. However, considering the
00282: expensive computational costs for evaluating the architecture, the signal is too sparse. In our NAS-
00283: Bench-201, we also provide some diagnostic information which is some extra statistics obtained
00284: during training each architecture. Collecting these statistics almost involves no extra computation
00285: cost but may provide insights for better designs and training strategies of different NAS algorithms,
00286: such as platform-aware NAS (Tan et al., 2019), accuracy prediction (Baker et al., 2018), mutation-
00287: based NAS (Cai et al., 2018; Chen et al., 2016), etc.
00288: Architecture Computational Costs: NAS-Bench-201 provides three computation metrics for each
00289: architecture тА?the number of parameters, FLOPs, and latency. Algorithms that target on searching
00290: architectures with computational constraints, such as models on edge devices, can use these metrics
00291: directly in their algorithm designs without extra calculations.
00292: Fine-grained training and evaluation information.NAS-Bench-201 tracks the changes in loss and
00293: accuracy of every architecture after every training epochs. These ямБne-grained training and evaluation
00294: information shows the tendency of the architecture performance and could indicate some attributes
00295: of the model, such as the speed of convergence, the stability, the over-ямБtting or under-ямБtting levels,
00296: etc. These attributes may beneямБt the designs of NAS algorithms. Besides, some methods learn to
00297: predict the ямБnal accuracy of an architecture based on the results of few early training epochs (Baker
00298: et al., 2018). These algorithm can be trained faster and the performance of the accuracy prediction
00299: can be evaluated using the ямБne-grained evaluation information.
00300: Parameters of optimized architecture. Our NAS-Bench-201 releases the trained parameters for
00301: each architecture. This can provide ground truth label for hypernetwork-based NAS methods (Zhang
00302: et al., 2019; Brock et al., 2018), which learn to generate parameters of an architecture. Other
00303: methods mutate an architecture to become another one (Real et al., 2019; Cai et al., 2018). With
00304: NAS-Bench-201, researchers could directly use the off-the-shelf parameters instead of training from
00305: scratch and analyze how to transfer parameters from one architecture to another.

00515: 6 D ISCUSSION
00516: How to avoid over-ямБtting on NAS-Bench-201? Our NAS-Bench-201 provides a benchmark for
00517: NAS algorithms, aiming to provide a fair and computational cost-friendly environment to the NAS
00518: community. The trained architecture and the easy-to-access performance of each architecture might
00519: provide some insidious ways for designing algorithms to over-ямБt the best architecture in our NAS-
00520: Bench-201. Thus, we propose some rules which we wish the users will follow to achieve the original
00521: intention of NAS-Bench-201, a fair and efямБcient benchmark.
00522: 1. No regularization for a speciямБc operation.Since the best architecture is known in our benchmark,
00523: speciямБc designs to ямБt the structural attributes of the best performed architecture are insidious ways to
00524: ямБt our NAS-Bench-201. For example, as mentioned in Section 5, we found that the best architecture
00525: with the same amount of parameters for CIFAR10 onNAS-Bench-201 is ResNet. Restrictions on the
00526: number of residual connections is a way to over-ямБt the CIFAR10 benchmark. While this can give a
00527: good result on this benchmark, the searching algorithm might not generalize to other benchmarks.
00528: 2. Use the provided performance. The training strategy affects the performance of the architecture.
00529: We suggest the users stick to the performance provided in our benchmark even if it is feasible to use
00530: otherH to get a better performance. This provides a fair comparison with other algorithms.
00531: 3. Report results of multiple searching runs. Since our benchmark can help to largely decrease the
00532: computational cost for a number of algorithms. Multiple searching runs give stable results of the
00533: searching algorithm with acceptable time cost.
00534: Limitation regarding to hyper-parameter optimization (HPO). The performance of an architec-
00535: ture depends on the hyper-parametersH for its training and the optimal conямБguration ofH may vary
00536: for different architectures. In NAS-Bench-201, we use the same conямБguration for all architectures,
00537: which may bring biases to the performance of some architectures. One related solution is HPO,
00538: which aims to search the optimal hyper-parameter conямБguration. However, searching the optimal
00539: hyper-parameter conямБgurations and the architecture in one shot is too computationally expensive
00540: and still is an open problem.
00541: Potential designs using diagnostic information in NAS-Bench-201. As pointed in Section 2.4,
00542: different kinds of diagnostic information are provided. We hope that more insights about NAS could
00543: be found by analyzing these diagnostic information and further motivate potential solutions for NAS.
00544: For example, parameter sharing (Pham et al., 2018) is the crucial technique to improve the searching
00545: efямБciency, but the shared parameter would sacriямБce the accuracy of each architecture. Could we ямБnd
00546: a better way to share parameters of each architecture from the learned 15,625 modelsтА?parameters?
00547: Generalization ability of the search space. It is important to test the generalization of observations
00548: on this dataset. An idea strategy is to do all benchmark experiments on a much larger search space.
00549: Unfortunately, it is prohibitive regarding the expensive computational cost. We bring some results
00550: from (Ying et al., 2019) and (Zela et al., 2020) to provide some preliminary evidence of generaliza-
00551: tion. In Figure 2, we show the rankings of RS, REA, and REINFORCE is ( REA > REINFORCE
00552: > RS ). This is consistent with results in NAS-Bench-101, which contains more architecture candi-
00553: dates. For NAS methods with parameter sharing, we ямБnd that GDASтЙ?DARTSтЙ?ENAS, which is
00554: also consistent with results in NAS-Bench-1SHOT1. Therefore, observations from our NAS-Bench-
00555: 201 may generalize to other search spaces.
00556: 7 C ONCLUSION & FUTURE WORK
00557: In this paper, we introduce NAS-Bench-201 that extends the scope of reproducible NAS. In NAS-
00558: Bench-201, almost any NAS algorithms can be directly evaluated. We train and evaluate 15,625
00559: architecture on three different datasets, and we provide results regarding different metrics. We
00560: comprehensively analyze our dataset and test some recent NAS algorithms on NAS-Bench-201 to
00561: serve as baselines for future works. In future, we will (1) consider HPO and NAS together and (2)
00562: much larger search space. We welcome researchers to try their NAS algorithms on our NAS-Bench-
00563: 201 and would update the paper to include their results.

00835: C D ISCUSSION FOR NAS WITH PARAMETER SHARING
00836: Parameter sharing (Pham et al., 2018) becomes a common technique to improve the efямБciency of
00837: differentiable neural architecture search methods (Liu et al., 2019; Dong & Yang, 2019b;a). The
00838: shared parameters are shared over millions of architecture candidates. It is almost impossible for the
00839: shared parameters to be optimal for all candidates. We hope to evaluate the trained shared parame-
00840: ters quantitatively. Specially, we use DARTS, GDAS, and SETN to optimize the shared parameters
00841: and the architecture encoding on CIFAR-10. For each architecture candidate, we can calculate its
00842: probability of being a good architecture from the architecture encoding following SETN (Dong &
00843: Yang, 2019a). In addition, we can also evaluate a candidate using the shared parameters on the val-
00844: idation set to obtain тАЬthe one-shot validation accuracyтА? It is computationally expensive to evaluate
00845: all candidates on the whole validation set. To accelerate this procedure, we evaluate each architec-
00846: ture on a mini-batch with the size of 2048, and use the accuracy on this mini-batch to approximate
00847: тАЬthe one-shot validation accuracyтА? Ideally, the architecture ranking sorted by the probability or the
00848: one-shot validation accuracy should be similar to the ground truth ranking. We show the correlation
00849: between the proxy metric and the ground truth validation accuracy in Table 7. There are several
00850: observations: (1) The correlation between the probability (encoded by the architecture encoding)
00851: and the ground truth accuracy is low. It suggests that the argmax-based deriving strategy (Liu et al.,
00852: 2019) can not secure a good architecture. It remains open on how to derive a good architecture after
00853: optimizing the shared parameters. (2) The behavior of BN layers is important to one-shot valida-
00854: tion accuracy. The accumulated mean and variance from the training set are harmful to one-shot
00855: accuracy. Instead, each architecture candidate should re-calculate the mean and variance of the BN
00856: layers. (3) GDAS introduced Gumbel-softmax sampling when optimizing the architecture encod-
00857: ing. This strategy leads to a high correlation for the learned probability than that of DARTS. (4) The
00858: uniform sampling strategy for training the shared parameters (Dong & Yang, 2019a) can increase
00859: the correlation for one-shot accuracy compared to the strategy of the joint optimizing strategy (Dong
00860: & Yang, 2019b; Liu et al., 2019).
